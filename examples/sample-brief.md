## Company snapshot

Acme Corporation is a workflow-automation platform for mid-market operations teams, founded in 2017 and headquartered in Denver. They sell almost exclusively into RevOps and Finance leaders at companies between 200 and 2,000 employees, with a land-and-expand motion from a single workflow into multi-team deployments. They closed a $48M Series C in late 2024 and grew headcount from 220 to 320 over the last 14 months, with most of the hiring in go-to-market roles. Recent product direction has shifted decisively toward AI-native automation — they shipped an LLM-powered workflow builder in March 2026 and have been talking about it publicly ever since.

## Their role

Jane Doe became VP of Revenue Operations at Acme in September 2025, reporting to the CRO. Before Acme she spent four years at HubSpot leading sales operations for the mid-market segment, and before that two years in finance at Stripe. She has a team of nine, including dedicated leads for sales enablement, comp design, and revenue analytics. The combination of a finance background and an operations seat suggests she'll evaluate any AI-tooling pitch through both an ROI lens and a workflow-fit lens — she has the credibility internally to push through new spend, but she'll want to see the numbers.

1. **New comp plan rolling out across the AE org.** Acme moved from quota-based to outcomes-based compensation in Q1 2026, and Jane has spoken publicly about the data-quality challenges the change exposed. This is fresh on her mind and is likely the lens she's evaluating new tooling through.

2. **Aggressive enablement hiring.** Acme posted four enablement and sales-ops roles in the last 60 days, including a Director of Enablement. That suggests headcount is already approved — the question is whether she's also evaluating tooling to scale faster than hires can ramp.

3. **Public commitment to AI-native ops.** The CEO has done two podcast appearances in the last 30 days framing Acme as "ops-team-first AI." Jane will be measured against that vision internally. A vendor that helps her show concrete AI wins inside her own function will land much harder than one that doesn't.

## Three opening questions

1. **"You shipped the new comp model in Q1 — what surprised you about how the AE team adapted?"**
   *Why this lands: it references the actual change, signals you've done your homework, and invites her to share where the friction is now.*

2. **"I saw the Director of Enablement listing went up last month — what does the org look like once that role is filled?"**
   *Why this lands: surfaces team structure and timing without pushing on tooling, and gives her a chance to describe what she's building toward.*

3. **"Your CEO has been pretty public about Acme being 'ops-team-first AI' — how does that show up in your day-to-day?"**
   *Why this lands: lets her connect external narrative to internal reality, which usually surfaces the gaps a vendor can fill.*

## Three discovery questions

1. **"Where in the AE onboarding flow today does manual work create the longest delays?"**
   *Listening for: a specific bottleneck (CRM hygiene, training content, ramp-time analytics) — the answer tells you which Sherlock-style tool would land first.*

2. **"How are you measuring whether the new comp plan is producing the behavior change you wanted?"**
   *Listening for: whether the measurement is happening at all, or whether they're flying blind. Either answer creates an opening.*

3. **"If you had to pick one thing in your function that you'd want an AI co-pilot doing six months from now, what would it be?"**
   *Listening for: the concrete pain at the top of her mind. This is the question that turns a discovery call into a scoping conversation.*

## Watch for

Jane is eight months into the role and just shipped a high-visibility comp change — exactly the moment when a VP either over-commits to new initiatives or holds the line. Don't push for a follow-up that requires three of her people to attend; she'll resist anything that taxes her team's bandwidth right now. Optimize the next step for **her** specifically — a 20-minute working session, a written audit, an async loom — not a multi-stakeholder demo.
