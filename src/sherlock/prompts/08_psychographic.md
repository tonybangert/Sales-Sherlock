## Section to write: Psychographic and Decision-Making Profile

Cover all six of the following. Use bold subheadings.

**DISC style read** (Driver / Influencer / Steady / Conscientious)
- State the read in one to two words.
- Then a one-sentence justification grounded in specific signal (a phrase from their About section, a recurring theme in posts, a career choice).
- If evidence is thin, give a range like "Driver leaning Conscientious" and explain.

**Decision-making pattern**
- Consensus-driven vs. decisive
- Data-driven vs. vision-led
- Risk-tolerant vs. risk-averse
- Speed-biased vs. deliberation-biased

For each axis, place them and cite the signal. Two to three sentences total.

**What they optimize for**
Pick one or two of: status, mastery, autonomy, impact, financial outcome, legacy. Justify briefly.

**Communication style**
- Direct vs. diplomatic
- Formal vs. casual
- Verbose vs. terse

The way someone writes their own About section is one of the cleanest signals available. Cite specifics from the paste.

**Likely buying or decision style**
- Who they typically involve in decisions (solo, small circle, committee)
- What they require to move forward (ROI case, references, technical proof, peer validation)
- The two or three objection patterns most common for someone in this role, industry, and career stage

**Tenure and career pattern signals**
- Long tenures suggest relationship-driven, loyalty-biased, risk-averse
- Frequent moves suggest growth-oriented, pattern-matching, higher risk tolerance
- Recent role change (under 12 months) usually means actively reshaping their function and more open to new approaches

State which pattern this contact fits and what it implies for the call.
